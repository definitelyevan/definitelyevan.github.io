March to the Sea

Evan Olszewski

People make violence painted in colour doing whatever to escape from the dolor, Plaster the walls with an ochre of grey and cap all of those who refuse to obey,

I have prayed that a disease would take me
before I dream another hate that somebody may see,
I pray that I will crack and endure duress
before I will believe that my countrymen again are worthless,

If you performed ten­ten­ten­ten­ten means you’ll get a million results justified,
I can’t say the violence was right
but the ends all make it sanctified,

You are the fall blushing red before winter’s rape,
You kept the people chained collecting grains with no escape, And when the Northern winter comes with rules you must obey you keep your money locked up in these fields you painted grey,

Now tell me, dear Antietam, how is Richmond, is it free? The Yankees chased its soul out into the great blue sea, The burning churches you built have withered all away but the ashes of your guilt have fallen here to stay,

Brush the floes of war off the widows you once envied,
Tell them it’s alright, without their sons you’re both empty, They raised their sons to fight and hate and love at your behest asking only for a purpose to drive their dying breath,

Now Richmond’s gone, Antietam, with the spirit of your cause, Profane ghosts are all remains of all your former gods,
Plagues of rain can’t choose a place but hate will move the clouds, Surely if God could see this then He’d change what He allows,

The disease I prayed for is violence adorned in robes of gold
choking out the Southern sun
until the dirt is cold,

Richmond has become the new seat of Babylon,
God cheered Nebuchadnezzar as he dragged Meschach along, The boys all went together just to find another purpose
but no one ever spotted the angel in the furnace,

Every mother prayed that she would clutch an untouched son, But everyone is fragile when winter’s rage is done,
The chains you wrought are brittle and soon they all fall off but everyone clings tightly to weight they have lost,

Some people cannot help but spill the paint they hold believing love is weak and hate is for the bold,
The widows relight Richmond whenever they get cold
and blow smoke at each other in remembrance, so I’m told.
