a chronologic

‘august xvii’ is a ‘chronologic’. a ‘chronologic’ is a made­up term to describe a book that keeps a record of the writings (and therefore, thought/feelings/etc.) of

a person during a given piece of time. the author lived in a place far away from his home for this book and the works are kept in the order of production here so they can accurately portray the chain of thought over the month of august ‘15.

flint moon, tinder box

A band of stars lashes the sky together, northwest, the wind and southeast, the dust

Even the ground slept beneath them, healing in their steady trust

Strikes of flint glittering in place, they are waves on a cosmic shore, burning and replacing in the ebb and flow

The tide washes in and out between each other, their middles cross to form the spray brilliance

That is darkness aglow and silence singing between two luminous points

the monarchy

Piles of meat gathered at my feet, thrown by the supplicants below. “Please,” they cried, “Please give me one more turn ­ one more chance.”

The daydream machine hummed nearby, myself and two heavily armed pig­men blocking the door. Rushing nearer, too near, to the door one man was struck down; the crowd erupted, not in fury, but excitement. “More meat, more meat,” chanting, “More meat to please the Man.”

Their claws tore apart the skin, and clutched the red red stuff, throwing it in my direction. Meat was rare, very rare. One of the man’s fingers would have sold for a month in today’s market. A month of indentured servitude, that is. Yet every scrap of the man was thrown at my feet, its odour singing to my nostrils as it reached room temperature on the cracked walk.

The chalky thunder of a sulfur storm crumbled through the yellow clouds draped over the ruins and the crowd didn’t even flinch.

I chose a boy who was not near the dais, just so I could watch the jealous looks of the parting mob as he reached the front. He looked in my eyes without hesitation and whispered the words into my nostrils. The fur on my neck bristled patiently when his eyes looked like mine.

He took the tongs to his own eyes, brave kid, and threw them through the door of the daydream machine himself. His meat fell to the surface of the platform, his soul lost between the gears, and I spoke, “It is consecrated.”

The people gawked and feasted, chanting, “Man, Man, Noble Man”.

about the poet

Evan Olsz is a young poet from the south. not the

dirty south, though. just the regular south. this normal­south poet has self­published two anthologies of unrelated poems because he can’t stay focused to bring together a chapbook worth of poems on a single subject.

Evan has been published in many indie lit mags including The Morning Bell Lit and online in Electric Cereal. He claims to be against ‘big magazine’ but critics expect his position to change once someone is willing to pay him for his work.

When not writing poems (which, to be fair, is a lot of time) Evan paints, runs, reads, and listens to very loud music. sometimes, he does at least two of those things simultaneously.

Likes include: the ocean, the railroad and all pertaining miscellany, and office supplies.
