aglow and silence singing between two luminous points . the monarchy Piles of meat gathered at my feet, thrown by the supplicants below. “Please,” they cried, “Please give me one more turn ­ one more chance.” The daydream machine hummed nearby, myself and two heavily armed pig­men blocking the door. Rushing nearer, too near, to the door one man was struck down; the crowd erupted, not in fury, but excitement. “More meat, more meat,” chanting, “More meat to please the Man.” Their claws tore apart the skin, and clutched the red red stuff, throwing it in my direction. Meat was rare, very rare. One of the man’s fingers would have sold for a month in today’s market. A month of indentured servitude, that is. Yet every scrap of the man was thrown at my feet, its odour singing to my nostrils as it reached room temperature on the cracked walk. The chalky thunder of a sulfur storm crumbled through the yellow clouds draped over the ruins and the crowd didn’t even flinch. I chose a boy who was not near the dais, just so I could watch the jealous looks of the parting mob as he reached the front. He looked in my eyes without hesitation and whispered the words into my nostrils. The fur on my neck bristled patiently when his eyes looked like mine. He took the tongs to his own eyes, brave kid, and threw them through the door of the daydream machine himself. His meat fell to the surface of the platform, his soul lost between the gears, and I spoke, “It is consecrated.” The people gawked and feasted, chanting, “Man, Man, Noble Man”. cos my loan A nation’s poet Evan Olsz is a young poet from the south. not the dirty south, though. just the regular south. this normal­south poet has self­published two anthologies of unrelated poems because he can’t stay focused to bring together a chapbook worth of poems on a single subject. Evan has been published in many indie lit mags including The Morning Bell Lit and online in Electric Cereal. He claims to be against ‘big magazine’ but critics expect his position to change once someone is willing to pay him for his work. When not writing poems (which, to be fair, is a lot of time) Evan paints, runs, reads, and listens to very loud music. sometimes, he does at least two of those things simultaneously. Likes include: the ocean, the railroad and all pertaining miscellany, and office supplies.
